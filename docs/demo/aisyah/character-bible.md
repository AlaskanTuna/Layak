# Aisyah Character Bible and Image Prompt Pack

Use this file as the source of truth when generating Aisyah-related visuals for the pitch deck, README banner variants, demo video overlays, or supporting marketing images.

The goal is consistency:

- same face and age across scenes
- same family situation across scenes
- same working-class Malaysian context across scenes
- no accidental drift into glamorous, generic, or Westernized stock-photo imagery

## Canonical Persona

- Name: `Aisyah binti Ahmad`
- Age: `34`
- Location: `Kuantan, Malaysia`
- Role: `self-employed e-hailing / gig driver`
- Tax profile: `Form B` filer, not salaried
- Monthly income: `about RM2,800`
- Household: `2 children`, ages `7` and `10`
- Elder dependant: `1 father`, age `70`
- Household size: `4`
- Digital literacy: moderate, practical, comfortable with phones and e-wallet apps
- Emotional posture: busy, financially careful, skeptical of complicated government forms, but capable and determined
- Product-story role: the primary Layak demo persona and the human face of fragmented public-service discovery

## Visual Identity

Keep these traits fixed as often as possible:

- Malaysian Malay woman, age 34
- warm medium-brown skin tone
- dark brown eyes
- soft oval face
- average build

- black hijab for consistency across scenes

Recommended recurring look:

- muted green, beige, grey, or navy clothing

### Style Summary

- soft, luminous editorial illustration rather than hard photorealism
- semi-realistic faces with slightly idealized features
- warm cream background with teal and gold accents in the hopeful scenes
- glowing translucent UI cards and document panels
- gentle light trails or civic-energy ribbons across the scene
- Malaysian cultural motifs in the background:
  floral geometry, skyline silhouettes, traditional house references, tropical foliage
- polished social-impact visual language
- hopeful, civic, trustworthy, human-centered tone

### Emotional Progression

The 5-minute demo script should be treated as the source of truth for scene mood.

Do not keep every scene equally bright, golden, or resolved.

Instead, the visual arc should move like this:

1. `Aisyah introduction`
   grounded, calm, human, slightly serious
2. `Problem / fragmentation`
   dimmer, more muted, a little heavier, busy and mentally burdened
3. `Portal overload / bill-and-form stress`
   duller, denser, visibly stressful, not hopeless but strained
4. `Layak flow begins`
   cleaner, more structured, gentle return of light and order
5. `Results / explanation / trust`
   brighter, clearer, more legible, more emotionally open
6. `Draft packet / final close`
   fully aligned with the current landing-page theme:
   warm cream, teal, and gold, hopeful and trustworthy

Push the early scenes harder than before:

- more subdued and darker
- less glow
- visibly more mental pressure
- still dignified, not melodramatic
- stressed, not broken

### Scene-Based Lighting Rules

Use these rules to avoid one-note visuals:

- early scenes:
  softer contrast, dimmer interiors, darker teal, dusty sage, grey-green, warm shadow, reduced background clarity
- stress scenes:
  much less gold glow, more paper clutter, denser composition, tired but composed face, dim translucent UI clutter, heavier haze
- transition scenes:
  introduce cleaner highlights and more breathing room
- ending scenes:
  restore the signature teal-and-gold civic glow from the landing page

If a scene is about burden, bureaucracy, or confusion:

- let the palette go darker than the default landing art
- compress the composition slightly so it feels more crowded
- use dim cream-grey backgrounds instead of bright cream
- keep green-teal civic tones, but mute and desaturate them
- keep gold almost absent until the hopeful stages

### Face and Expression Rules

- early scenes:
  neutral, slightly serious, or quietly worried expression
- difficulty scenes:
  focused, tired, concerned, mentally overloaded
- do not use:
  broad smiles during stress scenes
- outcome scenes:
  relief, confidence, calm hope
- final scenes:
  gentle optimism, not exaggerated celebration

### Color Palette

- early / burdened scenes:
  `darkened teal`, `muted sage`, `soft grey-green`, `cream-grey`, `warm beige shadow`
- stress scenes:
  `duller teal`, `paper tan`, `muted olive`, `dim cream-grey`, `very low amber highlights`
- hopeful ending scenes:
  `warm cream / ivory`, `soft teal / jade / sea-green`, `muted gold / amber glow`, `sage and pale turquoise`
- keep atmospheric haze soft throughout, but increase brightness gradually toward the ending

### Stress-First Variant

When the scene is specifically about burden, bills, repeated forms, or fragmented services, lean into this darker variant:

- subdued teal civic visual language
- muted low-contrast palette
- soft textured shadows
- heavier atmospheric haze
- dim translucent UI cards that feel cluttered rather than magical
- darker roadside or interior setting
- shoulders slightly slouched, phone held closer, more concerned expression
- no harsh contrast, no cinematic hero glow, no cheerful gold wash

### Composition Traits

- layered collage feeling: people + UI panels + civic landscape
- soft glow around cards, icons, or family-related symbols
- clean negative space where marketing copy could sit
- rounded forms, floating cards, and subtle depth
- no gritty realism, no harsh shadows, no corporate stock-photo style

### Best Fit for Aisyah

To stay consistent with the landing page, Aisyah should be rendered as:

- a stylized avatar or semi-realistic illustrated character
- not a literal photo
- not a cartoon with exaggerated proportions

Recommended phrasing:

- `stylized editorial illustration`
- `soft cinematic matte painting`
- `semi-realistic Malaysian character illustration`

## Consistency Rules for Image Models

To improve consistency across repeated generations:

- always reuse the `Master Prompt`
- or reuse the `Landing-Style Master Prompt` if matching the site artwork
- always include `34-year-old Malaysian Malay woman from Kuantan`
- always include `black hijab`
- always include `two school-age children and her 70-year-old father` when the scene is at home
- keep the tone either `realistic documentary` or `stylized editorial landing-page artwork`, but do not mix them randomly
- use one strong hero portrait as the visual anchor if your image tool supports reference-image conditioning

## What Not To Drift On

Avoid accidentally changing these facts across scenes:

- she is not salaried
- she is not a corporate office worker
- she is not wealthy
- she is not elderly
- she does not have only one child
- her elderly dependant is her `father`
- she is part of a four-person household
- she should feel like a real Malaysian citizen, not a polished stock model
