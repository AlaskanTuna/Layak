# Official FAQ

Version 1 - Updated: 14 April 2026

Organised by Google Developer Groups On Campus, Universiti Teknologi Malaysia

## General Information

### What is a "Build with AI" event?

Build with AI is a community-led series of events focused on helping developers create safe, secure, and scalable solutions using Google AI and Google Cloud technology. The program includes workshops, technical sessions, and hands-on project building.

### What will I learn at a Build with AI event?

- Hands-on experience with Gemini and other Google AI models.
- Integration with Firebase, Android, Flutter, and Google Cloud.
- Best practices and product updates from Google experts and community leaders.

### What are Google Developer Groups (GDG)?

GDGs are local, community-run meetups for developers interested in Google technologies. They offer opportunities to learn through workshops, connect with other developers, and build leadership skills.

### Who can attend these events?

Build with AI events are open to all developers, whether or not they are GDG members.

## Registration and Eligibility

### Who can register?

The hackathon is open to the public:

- Student Category: ages 16+ in Foundation, Undergraduate, Master's, or PhD programs.
- Open Category: ages 16+ beyond university, including professionals and hobbyists.
- International participation is allowed; no Malaysian representative is required.
- Team size is solo or up to 4 members.

### Do high school students qualify?

Yes, if they are 16 years old or above.

### Do all team members need to register?

No. Only the Team Lead registers for the team.

### What documents are required?

- GitHub profile links for all members.
- A standard 1-page professional resume from the Team Lead.

### Is there a confirmation letter?

No. Once the Team Lead registers and receives the Google Form response sheet, the team is eligible.

### Is a GitHub profile link required even if a member does not have one?

Yes. Every member must have a GitHub account and provide the link.

### What kind of resume does the Team Lead need to submit?

A standard 1-page professional resume, similar to one used for a job application. Canva templates are acceptable.

### Does the hacking period start immediately?

No. The official hacking period starts on 15 March 2026.

### Can I change my team members after registering?

Yes. Update the Google Form submission and email the changes to [myaifuturehackathon@gmail.com](mailto:myaifuturehackathon@gmail.com).

### If we are international students studying in Malaysia, which category do we fall under?

You fall under the Student Category, though you may choose the Open Category if you prefer.

## Tech Stack and Project Building

### What should I build?

Projects must align with the theme AI for Real Impact and use the Google AI ecosystem stack, such as Gemini API, Vertex AI, and Firebase Genkit.

The five tracks are:

- Agrotech and Food Security
- GovTech and Digital Services
- Healthcare and Wellbeing
- Smart Cities and Mobility
- FinTech and Security

### What is the deployment requirement?

- All projects must be hosted on Google Cloud Run.
- Final applications must be web-accessible.

### Can I use non-Google AI tools?

- Google AI Studio and Antigravity are the official tools recommended for coding assistance.
- If you integrate AI, it must use Gemini or Gemma and the Google AI ecosystem stack.
- Non-AI tools and frameworks are allowed.

### When are the important dates?

- Registration deadline: 20 April 2026, 11:59 PM (MYT).
- Submission deadline: 21 April 2026, 11:59 PM (MYT).
- Top 50 finalists announcement: 1 May 2026.
- Demo Day / Grand Finale: 16-17 May 2026.

### Are the sessions virtual or in person?

- Workshops and speaker sessions are online.
- The Top 50 finalists must attend the Final Event in person on 16-17 May 2026.

### Which requirements should I follow?

Always follow the [Official Participant Handbook](https://docs.google.com/document/u/0/d/1ufki6j__FJpbzeVEfMBJvZsFxUiEmywl4oJAiXlZwIQ/edit). The kickoff slides may not be fully updated.

### Does the final product have to be web-accessible?

Yes. The final application must be hosted on Google Cloud Run and accessible through the web.

### Can we develop a project for multiple tracks?

No. Each team must choose one track and build only for that track.

### Can we use FastAPI for backend routing in Cloud Run?

Yes, as long as the project still fulfills the mandatory Google AI stack requirements.

## Workshops and Google Cloud Credits

### Are workshop recordings available?

Yes. Recordings will be uploaded to [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@gdg_utm), but live attendance is encouraged for credit eligibility.

### How do I claim credits?

Credits are distributed through redemption links sent by email. Registration is required.

### Are credits individual or team-based?

Credits are given individually to each participant.

### What is the difference between workshop credits and hackathon credits?

- All participants receive 25 Google Cloud Credits during the preliminary round.
- Each live workshop attended gives participants 5 additional credits.
- Top 50 finalists receive another 25 credits during the Demo Day info session.

### Will certificates be provided?

Yes. Participants who make a successful submission will receive a Certificate of Participation.

### How and where can I redeem credits to access the Google Cloud SDK?

Credits are shared through a redemption link during live workshops.

### Can I claim workshop credit if I attended but did not register?

No. Registration is required.

### When is the special meet-up for the main hackathon credits?

The credit distribution is virtual. The exact date will be announced later.

### Where can I find the workshop feedback form?

The form is shared during the live session and closes shortly after the session ends.

### How many credits are allocated for the main hackathon, and who gets them?

All registered participants receive 25 credits. Redemption links are sent to the Team Lead's email.

### Can you publish a monthly workshop schedule in advance?

Not reliably. Workshop dates may change, and new sessions will be added to the handbook once confirmed.

## Submission Guidelines

### What is required for submission?

- A video demo of up to 3 minutes showing the working prototype.
- The final submission by 21 April 2026, 11:59 PM (MYT, GMT+8).

### What counts as a successful submission?

A successful submission must meet all of the following:

- The team registered before the deadline.
- The prototype is deployed on Google Cloud Run and accessible via the web.
- A 3-minute maximum video demo is uploaded.
- The project follows the official tech stack requirements, including Gemini and the Google AI ecosystem.

Only teams that meet all criteria will be evaluated.

### Can I use Canva instead of Google Slides for the pitch deck?

Yes.

### Should we put login credentials in the GitHub README?

No. Do not put credentials in the README. If your app requires login, submit test credentials through the Google Form provided by the organizers.

### Where can we find the project submission link?

The official link is shared in the #submission-prep Discord channel and through the submission form provided by the organizers.

### Is the submission deadline fixed?

Yes. There will be no extension.

## Demo Day and Finals

### When is Demo Day?

16-17 May 2026.

### Do all team members need to attend?

- Physical presence is mandatory during the assigned pitching slot.
- Attendance at the opening and closing ceremonies is strongly encouraged.

### How were finalists chosen?

Finalists were selected evenly across the five tracks in both categories:

- Student Category: 25 teams total.
- Open Category: 25 teams total.
- Total finalists: 50 teams.

### Does every team member need to speak during the pitch?

No. One presenter is fine, though full-team participation usually makes the pitch stronger.

### Will travel grants be provided?

The organizers are trying to secure travel grants and accommodation, but nothing is guaranteed.

### Where will Demo Day be held?

It will be held physically at UTM Skudai.

### Can an international finalist present online?

No. Final pitching is offline only.

### Are prizes awarded?

Yes. The prize pool is split across Student and Open categories:

| Prize Rank | Student Category                    | Open Category                    |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| 1st Place  | RM 2,500, National Student Champion | RM 5,000, National Open Champion |
| 2nd Place  | RM 1,500, Student 1st Runner-Up     | RM 3,500, Open 1st Runner-Up     |
| 3rd Place  | RM 500, Student 2nd Runner-Up       | RM 2,000, Open 2nd Runner-Up     |

All participants who submit a project will receive a Certificate of Participation.
