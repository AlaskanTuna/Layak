## Introduction

Project 2030: MyAI Future Hackathon is a national-scale hackathon focused on building practical AI solutions for Malaysia's major challenges. The program is run by GDG On Campus UTM with support from Google.

The goal is simple: move participants from consuming technology to building with it. Teams are expected to use the Google AI ecosystem and produce a working, web-accessible solution deployed on Google Cloud Run.

## Grand Challenges

Teams must choose one track and build within that scope only.

### Track 1: Padi and Plates (Agrotech and Food Security)

**Background:** Malaysia currently faces a multi-billion ringgit food import dependency, leaving the nation vulnerable to global supply chain fluctuations. Smallholder farmers, who form the backbone of this sector, suffer significant yield losses due to undetected crop pathologies and inefficient resource planning.

### Track 2: Citizens First (GovTech and Digital Services)

**Background:** The transition to "End-to-End" digital government services is a core pillar of the MyDIGITAL blueprint. Despite progress, bureaucratic friction and manual document verification remain significant bottlenecks, while climate change has made annual floods a recurring national crisis.

### Track 3: Vital Signs (Healthcare and Wellbeing)

**Background:** As Malaysia transitions toward "Aged Society" status, the public healthcare system faces unprecedented strain. Overcrowding in Emergency Departments and a shortage of home-care monitoring for the elderly are critical issues requiring immediate technological intervention.

### Track 4: Green Horizon (Smart Cities and Mobility)

**Background:** Urban centers, particularly the Johor-Singapore Innovation Corridor, face severe mobility congestion. Achieving Net Zero emissions by 2050 requires a fundamental shift in how transport systems are managed and how carbon footprints are tracked.

### Track 5: Secure Digital (FinTech and Security)

**Background:** Digital fraud and sophisticated scams result in millions of ringgit in losses for Malaysian citizens annually. Building a resilient digital economy requires advanced, real-time protection layers that can outpace increasingly automated threats.

## Technical Mandate

Every project must integrate the Google AI ecosystem as its core foundation. A valid project should go beyond chat and use AI to take action.

Required stack components:

1. **The Intelligence (Brain):** Gemini (Flash for speed/low-latency or Pro for complex reasoning).
2. **The Orchestrator:** Vertex AI Agent Builder and Firebase Genkit for building Agentic AI workflows.
3. **The Development Lifecycle:** Google Cloud Workstations for coding and Google Cloud Run for serverless deployment.
4. **The Context:** Vertex AI Search for grounded Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) using national or industrial datasets.

Tools and deployment:

- Use Google AI Studio or Google Antigravity.
- Deploy the final application on Google Cloud Run.

> **NOTE:** As per organizer, choice of agentic coding tool is totally optional but must be disclosed transparently in README.

## Rules and Regulations

Registration is free. By registering, all participants agree to the rules below.

### Eligibility

- Student Category: ages 16+ in Foundation, Undergraduate, Master's, and PhD.
- Open Category: ages 16+ beyond university, including professionals and hobbyists.
- Participants may join only one team.
- Registration is open to Malaysians who meet the stated criteria.

### Team Requirements

1. Teams may have 1 to 4 members.
2. Each team must designate one Team Lead.
3. Team composition is fixed after the registration deadline on 20 April 2026.
4. Mentors and advisors may guide teams but may not directly contribute to code, design, or submission materials.

### Submission Guidelines

1. Submission deadline is 21 April 2026 at 11:59 PM. No extensions.
2. All deliverables must be submitted through the official portal.
3. Required submission package:
   - Public GitHub repository with source code, README, and setup instructions.
   - Video demo up to 3 minutes, hosted on YouTube or Google Drive.
   - Google Slides deck, exported as PDF, up to 15 slides.
   - Google Cloud Run deployment link that works without login, or test credentials if login is unavoidable.
4. Submissions must be in English.
5. Projects must be built during the hackathon window starting on 15 March 2026.

### Code of Conduct and Plagiarism

1. Plagiarism is not allowed.
2. AI-generated code must be disclosed in the README.
3. Respectful conduct is required at all times.
4. Harassment and discrimination are not tolerated.
5. Top 50 finalists must attend both days of the Final Event in person.
6. Projects must follow ethical AI principles.

### Intellectual Property

1. Teams keep ownership of their work.
2. By submitting, teams grant Project 2030 and its sponsors a non-exclusive, royalty-free licence to showcase the project.
3. Open-source is encouraged but not required.
4. Third-party assets must be properly licensed.

### Disqualification Conditions

1. Late submission.
2. More than 4 team members at submission time.
3. Plagiarism or undisclosed reuse of another team's work.
4. Projects not built during the hackathon window.
5. Code of conduct violations.
6. Undisclosed ineligible participants.
7. Failure of Top 50 finalists to attend the final event in person.

### Prizes and Judging Disclaimer

1. Prize values may change before the final event.
2. Judges' decisions are final.
3. Prizes are awarded to the team, not individuals.
4. Track-specific awards may be given at the judges' discretion.
5. Prize recipients are responsible for any applicable taxes or levies.
